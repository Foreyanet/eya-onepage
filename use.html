How to Use Yi-Ya

Yi-Ya can be used as a lightweight research framework to structure observation, description, and comparison of early speech onset. No specific tools, software, or centralized protocol are required.

Who Yi-Ya Is For

  • Researchers studying early vocal development or speech onset (0–3 years).
  • Graduate students designing exploratory or longitudinal studies.
  • Labs seeking cross-study comparability without changing their core methods.

What Using Yi-Ya Requires

  • Clear documentation of early vocal observations.
  • Consistent descriptive units across participants or time points.
  • Transparent reporting of observation context and selection criteria.

What Using Yi-Ya Does Not Require

  • No use of a specific app or platform.
  • No submission of data to a central repository.
  • No approval or coordination with the Yi-Ya initiator.

Example: A Non-App Yi-Ya Study

A doctoral student conducts monthly home-based audio recordings of infants aged 6–24 months. Vocalizations are segmented and described using a small set of consistent observational categories (e.g., syllable shape, repetition, stability over time).

Yi-Ya is used to frame how these observations are reported and compared across participants, allowing later dialogue with other studies using similar descriptive logic, without sharing data or methods.

Using Yi-Ya Over Time

Researchers may adopt Yi-Ya partially or incrementally. The framework is designed to support exploratory work and gradual refinement rather than fixed experimental outcomes.
