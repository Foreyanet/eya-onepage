How to Refer to Yi-Ya

This page provides guidance on how to refer to Yi-Ya in academic papers, theses, preprints, and research reports.

General Principle

Yi-Ya is intended to be referred to as a shared research framework, not as a dataset, software tool, or experimental protocol.

Recommended Reference Language

Researchers may use formulations such as:

“This study adopts the Yi-Ya research framework, a shared observational and comparative framework for early speech onset (0–3 years), to structure the description and comparison of early vocal units across participants.”
“Our observational dimensions are informed by the Yi-Ya framework, which defines a method-neutral and cross-study comparable approach to early speech onset.”

These formulations may be adapted to fit disciplinary style and journal conventions.

Citation

Yi-Ya does not require citation to a single paper. When appropriate, authors may cite the project website:

Yi-Ya Research Framework
https://www.eya.net

Authorship and Attribution

Use of the Yi-Ya framework does not imply co-authorship, endorsement, or prior approval. Researchers retain full ownership of their study design, data, analysis, and conclusions.

Contact (Optional)

Contact with the Yi-Ya initiator is not required. Researchers may choose to reach out only if they wish to discuss cross-study alignment or contribute to framework refinement.
